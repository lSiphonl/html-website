Why I Enjoy Video Games

Video games have always been one of my favourite ways to spend time, not just for entertainment, but because they often represent complex systems, storytelling and skill based challenges all combined into one experience. What I find most interesting is how different genres focus on completely different aspects of experience, from strategy and reflexes to exploration and narrative.

I tend to enjoy games that either challenge me mentally, immerse me in a story, or allow me to explore detailed open worlds. Some games focus on difficulty and mastery, while others focus on creativity or teamwork, and I find value in all of these depending on my mindset at the time.

What Kind of Games Do I Enjoy?

I generally enjoy a mix of genres

  • Story driven games with deep narrative and world building
  • Open world games where exploration and freedom is a key part
  • Co-op games where teamwork and communication matters
  • Competitive multiplayer games that require focus and skill
  • RPG games with progression systems and character building
  • Horror games...

What makes games interesting to me is how they combine rules, systems and interaction into something that feels alive. Even when the mechanics are complex, the best games make them intuitive through experience, and learning those systems is part of what makes them enjoyable.

Some of My Favourite Titles

  • Elden Ring
  • Skyrim
  • Apex Legends
  • Dark Souls
  • Forza Horizon
  • Valorant
  • Resident Evil: Village

Each of these games offers something different. Some focus on difficulty and mastery, others on exploration or competition, but all of them share one thing in common for me, they reward learning their systems and improving over time. That is usually what keeps me engaged long after I start playing.

Games are not just a form of entertainment for me. They are interactive systems that challenge the way I think, react and adapt, whilst allowing me to unwind - The perfect combination imo.
